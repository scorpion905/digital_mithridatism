Digital Mithridatism

The Frictionless Trap

Despite its aging, the digital landscape remains a veiled mystery for most. People’s access to its basic features is now easier than ever. But in order for technology to lower its barrier of entry, sacrifices had to be made. Personal computers and laptops are used less and less as phones and tablets appear to be more attractive. Navigating inside iOS, iPadOS and Android is very easy, the OS’s having the least amount of friction compared to MacOS, Windows and the myriad of Linux distros plaguing the market. As such, the average user understands only the bare minimum they need in order to achieve their goals and complete their tasks, they neither understand nor look for the “why” and the “how” of its inner workings. They know how to open a browser and they barely understand how to look for things.

More and more people have heard that various Silicon Valley giants are using their data to form a digital profile of this individual, akin to Serial Experiments Lain, an avatar, a mask that’s both window and mirror, whose purpose is to look as much as it can like you. Our data is being sold, of that much most have become aware and after a 15 second outrage, apathetic to. But what if you could bring the value of your data close to zero, or even better, invert its value?

CYP450

Mithridatism is the practice of ingesting poison in non-lethal quantities in order to boost the body’s tolerance. Its name comes from king Mithridates VI, which consumed on a daily basis a micro dose of toxins, living in constant fear of being poisoned, after his father met his end by being poisoned.

Dokuhime (dooku - poison, hime - princess) is a manga that cauldrons mithridatism along with Chinese folklore and myths regarding poisoned concubines and transposes this poison into children in order to showcase a story of stolen childhood, weaponised children, the body as a political instrument and Schopenhauer’s hedgehog dilemma (the closer we come to one another the more likely we are to hurt each other).

The Alchemy of Noise

Combining these two concepts, which are two eons older than the first computer seems like it wouldn’t yield any meaningful benefits to our data theft conundrums. But what if you poisoned yourself, became the poison? Then that might poison the mask as well. Data poisoning refers to an attack in which a bad actor or adversary introduces malicious values in order to alter a data set or the behavior of a language model or an algorithm. It’s not about occasionally clicking an ad you despise, it requires a systematic, adversarial approach. A behavioral shift from the passivity of the consumer to an active obfuscator. This might look like deploying automated background scripts that constantly inject noise into the input pipeline, scraping unrelated topics, clicking conflicting ideologies, and downloading arbitrary files so that the algorithms can no longer parse the signal from the noise.

To become the poison requires a deliberate methodology of contamination. The modern algorithm is a sophisticated sorting mechanism, it thrives on patterns, categorizing the predictability of human habits, and humans are far more predictable than we’d like to admit. Defeating it sounds simple enough, break the pattern. This isn’t achievable through sheer silence as disconnecting is a privilege none can afford in the modern world, and a sudden digital blackout merely flags an individual as a high-priority anomaly, a much more useful resource to perfect the algorithm on. Instead, one solution is targeted saturation.

We can imagine a script, so as to not actively do something as users, a few lines of code running quietly in the background, acting as a proxy. While we sleep, this headless browser navigates the web, layering discordant data points onto their profile. It searches for industrial solar inverters, then queries obscure medieval literature, it lingers on extreme right-wing political manifestos before abruptly looking up donations to an anarcho-communist collective. It is the deliberate construction of an incoherent algorithmic Shadow.

The goal is to render the profile mathematically useless. The user introduces abrasive, contradictory variables, a harsh tobacco sting dropped into a bouquet of roses.

This form of mithridatism must extend beyond mere web searches. Obfuscation needs to bleed into the hardware itself by feeding dummy location coordinates to weather apps, altering the output pipeline of a device’s camera so that CV (computer vision) algorithms might isolate movement, but fail to recognize the static context of the room. You become nonsense in the machine’s eyes and logic. By gorging yourself on curated toxins, your mask swells into an unrecognizable caricature. The dataset is poisoned, its value inverted, and the tracker is left gazing into the Malkavian mirror.

The Isolation of the Carrier

The fundamental difference between poison and venom is represented via the delivery method of the toxin. Venom is actively injected into a victim via a bite, sting, or a claw, requiring a wound in order to function effectively. On the other hand, poison is passively delivered through ingestion, inhalation or absorption through the skin. This would generally place venom as an offensive tool and poison as a defensive one. Unfortunately, we are not poisonous by default, and an issue arises in the successful attempt at becoming poisonous. When you become the poison, the first casualty is not the algorithm, it is your own digital reflection. To constantly generate this synthetic noise you eventually split the self. You are no longer organically navigating the world, you are perpetually managing a decoy.

Psychologically, this becomes a heavy, creeping burden. It echoes a kind of Kierkegaardian despair that in trying so desperately to protect the authentic self from being consumed and quantified by the system, you lose yourself in a labyrinth of your own making. The mask, which used to be a somewhat accurate if invasive mirror, is artificially inflated into a grotesque Jungian Shadow made of static. You have saved your data, but you are now tethered to a ghost that behaves like sanity’s requiem (akin to the man and the Steppenwolf / concept of Ghost and Shell – Ghost in the Shell).

The true tragedy lies in the consequence of success. When the poisoning is complete, the mask is dead and the tracker is blinded. Tech giants can no longer commodify your underlying Cartesian core, your quiet, thinking, authentic self remains untouched and more importantly unmapped. Yet, in blinding the tracker you also blind the world to your presence.

This is where Schopenhauer’s hedgehog dilemma materializes in the modern age. We initially sought the warmth of the binary ecosystem, the tailored convenience, the instant resonance, the frictionless communities. But the closer we came to the machine, the more its surveillance pierced us. So, we became poison, grew spikes, deployed scripts, obfuscated inputs, turned our exterior abrasive and toxic.

We are protected, but the cost of privacy is isolation. The algorithms, now choking on our poisoned data, can no longer serve us. The internet reverts to a cold, untailored wasteland. The map is of no use to us anymore, it doesn’t know where we want to go, social media no longer knows who we might know, the feed doesn’t know what we want to read. By exacerbating toxicity, we ensure that the system can never again consume us, but we also ensure that in the vast, interconnected web, we remain impossibly and overwhelmingly alone.

Synthesis

With the heavy silence of the poisoned profile, the sheer, overwhelming isolation of Schopenhauer’s dilemma realized online becomes increasingly difficult to ignore. You’ve achieved sovereignty over your data, but you are left to wander a ghost town of your making. Yet, within this haunting finality lies a profound pivot, moth to butterfly, ash to Phoenix, death to life.

The poison killed the mask, but it was already suffocating you. The algorithm was a frictionless, artificial Eden, and by poisoning your data, you forced your own expulsion from it. Now, stripped of the avatar, you are forced to step out of the panopticon. The internet turns grayscale in order for the physical world to become vivid once more. Standing in fresh silence, you are free to choose: turn off the scripts to begin forging a new mask, or step out into the real world to seek authentic connections, the kind that aren’t curated by predictive models, but are forged through the friction, emotional depth and rational complexity of two real people.
